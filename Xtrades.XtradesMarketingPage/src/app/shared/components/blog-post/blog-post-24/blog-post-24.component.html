By Omar G @ Xtrades

When people new to stock trading come to Xtrades, a growing community of stocks and options traders, they're often looking for information about penny stocks.

Penny stocks, in case you aren't familiar, are typically stocks for companies that have a low valuation or companies whose fortunes have fallen on hard times. The stocks might be trading for less than a dollar a share, or even fractions of a penny in extreme cases.

If the stock is listed on a big exchange like NASDAQ and it stays under a price of $1 a share for too long, it can be de-listed. Companies with stock that languishes at under a penny a share sometimes end up on OTC (Over-the-Counter) exchanges. These stocks can be risky to share and some brokerages add fees to trading them that amateur traders might not even be aware are being charged to their account.

At Xtrades, many traders don't ignore penny stocks in favor of stocks from more valuable companies such as Tesla (TSLA) or Apple (APPL). There are some real advantages to trading penny stocks, even though the risk can be higher.

As Kevin Wan, the founder of Xtrades, says that penny stocks are a good way for inexperienced traders to get their feet wet and start learning how to trade, "People are drawn to instant gratification and penny stocks can be a provider for this. It is a more active form of playing the markets.

"I think penny stocks are a great way for traders to learn the markets, understand things through the lens of volume and on a shorter time frame, without the complexities of things like expiration date, or time decay of trading options."

Buying stock in a company just means owning a share of that company, whether it's Tesla or Apple or a much-smaller startup, but there are different types of stocks and they vary in price, voting rights and dividends, the payouts that companies make to shareholders when they're successful.

Mullen Automotive, Inc. (MULN) is an example of a penny stock that is popular with traders. (Image: TradingView)

Penny stocks in sectors including artificial intelligence, where there are literally hundreds of new companies emerging in this hot technology area; cannabis, in which a huge industry is being built as legalization efforts continue; and biotech, which can include medical-device and pharmaceutical firms; can be exciting to trade. Some of the reasons why include:

  • Volatility. Unlike large-cap stocks, a news announcement or earnings report can cause a penny stock to double or triple its value in one trading day. Of course, that risk goes both ways; an investment in a penny stock can become worthless overnight. Because these stocks trade in much lower volumes than higher-cap stocks, a few large transactions can move the stock sharply up or down. That also means an individual trader or a small group of them can also manipulate these stocks for their own gains.
  • Low barrier to entry. You may not have hundreds of dollars to invest in Nvidia and don't want to buy fractional shares of a stock, which some brokers offer. With penny stocks, you might be able to buy hundreds of shares of a stock for less than $50.
  • Getting in early. Some penny stocks belong to companies that are just starting. For an emerging industry like AI, you might get lucky and find some startups that have a lot of potential. While lots of penny stocks are traded short-term as day trades, some traders hold on to penny stocks longer-term as a low-cost bet on future growth.

Traders who decide they want to play in penny stocks have a lot of websites and tools to choose from, but it's hard to figure out what stocks are worth buying and even harder to decide when to enter or exit a trade.

Xtrades has built a successful Discord server and web + mobile app around the knowledge of its analysts and its big community of traders.

Experts such as Xtrades analyst Bahamut regularly post alerts on Xtrades, letting members know the best time to enter a new penny-stock trade, and even more importantly, when to get out of a trade to maximize profit and avoid losses. Bahamut says that traders who follow penny stocks look for recognizable chart patterns; when enough traders are drawn to a penny stock, that creates volume, which can drive up its price.

But product announcements and other company milestones can also help. "Volume usually comes in on stocks that are releasing news, whether it is quarterly earnings or other filings, or other catalysts such as FDA approvals, for example," he says. "This sets the stage for quality penny stock day trades."

Chart patterns that may take longer to play out can make penny stocks appropriate for swing trades, or trades that are held longer than a day. Those, he says, can net 20 to 50-percent gains.

To choose what stocks to pick, Bahamut says he relies on tools including Benzinga in premarket trading and Finviz for intraday trading for their screeners and scanners. He uses filters that measure things like float (how many shares are available for the public to trade), market cap and volatility.

"At that point," he says, "your list should be narrowed down heavily and you can analyze each option on your charting software." Charting software can include visual indicators that can help track buying and selling trends over time, volume, and other information that advanced traders use.

Bahamut says that new traders tackling penny stocks should "Trade lightly and carefully. Volatility is high, and adjusting to a new trading strategy takes time and patience. Focus on your process and don't overthink the results. Find out what works for you and perfect it, using that strategy up until it no longer works."
